1.
Salar Jung Museum		 Dar-ul-Shifa, Hyderabad,
Telangana, India
Salar Jung Museum is the third largest museum in India. It is the most famous museum in Hyderabad. Initially, it was used to house the personal art collection of the Salar Jung dynasty. The antiques in this museum date back to the first century, and each piece attests to the glorious culture of the ancient era of Hyderabad. You can find a varied collection of Aurangzeb's weapons, Jahangir's jade-crafted daggers, historical books, musical clocks, manuscripts, and several copies of the Quran written in gold and silver in various languages and fonts.
2.
Sudha Car Museum		 19-5-15, 1/D, Bahadurpura X Rd, APHB Conlony, Bahadurpura, Hyderabad, Telangana 500064
Sudha Car Museum was initially curated as a hobby and is a splendid automobile museum in Hyderabad. There is a vast display of unique cars resembling familiar objects from everyday life. Mr. K. Sudhakar makes all these cars, and this museum finds its place in the Limca Book of Records. There are cars shaped like helmets, beds, stilettos, lipsticks, handbags, etc. These cars are built using scrap material and are fully functional. With each of these cars, you can find a note with information regarding how the car was built; the duration taken to make it, and its speed. This museum is a favorite of kids, tech freaks, and automobile enthusiasts.
3.
Archaeology Museum		 I Floor, H A C A Bhavan, P G Road, 5-10-193, Srinagar - Kanyakumari Hwy, near Control Room, Lakdikapul, Hyderabad, Telangana 500004
The Telangana State Archaeology Museum is located inside the Hyderabad Public Garden. This museum gives you an insight into the culture of the two Telugu states. Archaeologist Henry Cousens discovered this museum in the 19th century. It was formerly known as Andhra Pradesh Archeology Museum or Hyderabad Museum. This museum helped in preserving the heritage of the region against the British. The efforts of the last Nizam of Hyderabad brought this museum into being. The structure houses decorative art pieces, arms, coins, Jain and Buddhist relics, bronze and stone sculptures, and other valuable stuff. This is Hyderabad's oldest museum and a must-visit place in the city. Make sure you see the 2000-year-old Egyptian mummy that is the highlight here.
4.
The Nizam’s Museum		 Purani Haveli Rd, Purani Haveli, Darulshifa, Hyderabad, Telangana 500002
The Nizam's Museum was in the Purani Haveli of Hyderabad and opened to the general public in 2000. It is one of the best museums in Hyderabad as you can see exquisite mementos, gift items, and souvenirs presented to Osman Ali Khan, the last Nizam of Hyderabad, by Asaf Jah VII in the year 1936 during his silver jubilee celebrations. The museum also features the Golden Throne of the Nizam, a gold tiffin box engraved with diamonds, and a glass painting by Mir Osman Ali. You can also see the wardrobe collection and other regal belongings of the sixth Nizam of Hyderabad. There is also a display of the vintage car collection of the Nizam, including a Rolls Royce 1930 model, a Jaguar Mark V, and a Packard.
5.
Kalakriti Art Gallery		 Plot 8-2, 465/1, Road No 4, near Hyatt Place, Banjara Hills, Hyderabad, Telangana 500034
While Hyderabad is celebrated for its ancient heritage, the Kalakriti Art Gallery proves it also fosters contemporary talent beautifully. Established in 2007, it has already gained fame as one of India’s finest private art galleries promoting avant garde creativity across varied mediums - from vivid canvases to innovative installations. Spread across three floors near Snow World, it houses multiple exhibition spaces hosting thematic shows by established and emerging artists. Undoubtedly ranking amongst popular museums in Hyderabad, Kalakriti stays true to its mission of ‘bringing art to life’ by providing promising local artists the forum to get discovered. Having already hosted over 100 shows, it continues its pursuit of identifying rare talent and niche concepts. Visitors keen on buying displayed pieces as art investors or collectors can purchase select art directly as well.
